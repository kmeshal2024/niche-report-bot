# How to promote your newsletter

## 1. Use the landing page

- **File:** `landing.html` in this repo.
- **Edit:** Replace `https://yoursubdomain.beehiiv.com/subscribe` with your real Beehiiv subscribe URL (in the `<a href="...">` links and in the `SUBSCRIBE_URL` in the script at the bottom). Replace the Unsubscribe and Privacy links if you have them.
- **Optional:** Change the title and tagline to match your niche (e.g. keep "Saudi Construction Tenders" or use your own name).
- **Host:** Put the file online so you can share a link:
  - **GitHub Pages:** Push the repo, enable Pages, set source to main branch and root (or /docs if you put landing.html there).
  - **Netlify / Vercel:** Drag the folder or connect the repo; point to the file or a folder containing it.
  - **Beehiiv:** You can also create a "Subscribe" or "Landing" post and paste in a simplified version of the HTML (hero + CTA).

## 2. Where to promote

- **LinkedIn:** Post a short “why I started this” plus one key takeaway from a recent issue. Link to your landing page or Beehiiv subscribe form. Post consistently (e.g. every Monday when the issue goes out).
- **X (Twitter):** Same idea — one insight or number from the digest + link. Use 1–2 relevant hashtags (e.g. #SaudiConstruction #Vision2030 #Tenders).
- **Industry / WhatsApp groups:** If you’re in contractor or project-manager groups, share the link once with a one-line pitch (“Weekly Saudi construction tenders digest – subscribe if you want it in your inbox”).
- **Email:** If you have a small list (colleagues, ex-clients), one personal email: “I’m sending a weekly digest on Saudi tenders; here’s the link if you’d like to subscribe.”
- **Partners:** Ask one or two trusted contacts to share the link with their network or post it once.

## 3. What to say (messaging)

- **Headline:** “Weekly Saudi construction tenders digest” or “Key tenders and opportunities every Monday.”
- **One-liner:** “One email a week with the tenders and developments that matter — no fluff.”
- **Social post template:** “New issue of [Newsletter Name] is out: [one concrete takeaway, e.g. ‘Expo 2030 metro tender, NWC water contracts, and new contractor exemptions.’] Subscribe here: [link].”

## 4. Make it easy to say yes

- Landing page has one main CTA: **Subscribe free**.
- Say “Every Monday” and “Unsubscribe anytime” to reduce friction.
- Optional: add “No spam” or “Only the digest, nothing else” near the button.

## 5. Track and iterate

- In Beehiiv, watch opens and clicks. If opens are low, test a stronger subject line (e.g. include a specific tender or number).
- If almost no one clicks from social to subscribe, try a clearer CTA on the post (“Subscribe here” + link) or a short thread explaining what’s in the newsletter, then link at the end.

## 6. Consistency

- Send on the same day (e.g. every Monday). Consistency builds habit and trust.
- Promote the same way regularly (e.g. one LinkedIn post per issue) so people who see you often can decide to subscribe.
